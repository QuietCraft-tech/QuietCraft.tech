Your Post Title Goes Here

15 June 2026 4 min read Shafquat

This is the opening paragraph of your post. Write it as you would speak — clear, unhurried, and without jargon. The font is set for comfortable reading at around 65 characters per line.

A second paragraph. You can use bold for emphasis and italics for titles or light stress. Keep paragraphs short — three to five sentences is a good length.

A Section Heading

Use <h2> for major sections and <h3> for sub-sections. Headings are set in Cormorant Garamond, which gives them a different weight from body text and makes the structure easy to scan.

Pull quotes work well for a key idea you want to linger on. Keep them to one or two sentences — they're meant to slow the reader down, not stop them entirely.

Continue the post after the blockquote. You can add links like this one inline.

A Smaller Sub-section

  • Use lists sparingly — prose reads better than bullets in most cases.
  • But sometimes a list is the right tool.
  • Keep each item to one or two lines.

A horizontal rule (<hr>) creates a soft break between major topics within a post. Use it when a new section feels like a fresh thought rather than a continuation.

End with something worth remembering — a quiet observation, a next step, or simply where your thinking has landed. No call to action required.
